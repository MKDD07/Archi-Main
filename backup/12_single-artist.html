Project Overview

Sprouts is one of India’s most iconic public art installations, conceived and crafted by Indian artist and designer Vibhor Sogani. Commissioned by the Government of Delhi as part of an urban beautification initiative, this monumental work is set amidst the greens surrounding the AIIMS flyover in the heart of New Delhi.

The installation consists of large, sculptural forms reminiscent of emerging shoots or seeds breaking through the earth. Rising up to 40 feet high and spread over 6 acres, these stainless steel elements act as a visual metaphor for growth, renewal and transformation.

Design Intent & Significance

Sited at a major intersection in the city, Sprouts was conceptualized to reflect New Delhi’s transformation into a global capital — a place where tradition and modernity intersect. The work was developed following a brief to express “From walled city to world city”.

The organic forms evoke natural growth while introducing an abstract, artistic language into the urban environment. Reflective surfaces interact with light and weather, creating shifting impressions throughout the day.

Urban Impact

As a landmark public sculpture, Sprouts has become synonymous with the visual identity of this part of New Delhi. Its presence transforms an everyday transport corridor into a destination for contemplation and civic pride.

An alumnus of NID, Vibhor Sogani is India’s premier installation artist. His work is a dialogue between industrial materials and the ethereal quality of light.
